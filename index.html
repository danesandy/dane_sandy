Mr. Dane Sandy holds a BSc. As a junior, Mr. Sandy was a national athlete and represented his country for the 100m and 200m sprints. From this age he learned the valuable discipline of setting goals, making plans and going the extra mile to reach his targets.

He is naturally gifted at mathematics and problem solving. Although he has won multiple top place awards for mathematics and additional mathematics at the high school level, one of the most concrete examples of his problem-solving strength was in winning the UWI open scholarship exam. The examination is akin to a more intense version of the SATs, consisting of tricky logic, mathematics and grammar questions to test aptitude. It is offered to both first year and incoming students and only the top two persons per faculty are selected. Mr. Sandy won for the faculty of engineering (1466 enrolled students along with incoming students).
